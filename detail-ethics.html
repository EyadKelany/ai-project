Core Pillars

Ethics & Safety Deep Dives

Technical and governance frameworks for each pillar of responsible AI.

Fairness & Bias Mitigation

Measuring and reducing systematic disparities across protected groups.

  • Metrics: Demographic parity, equalized odds, equal opportunity, calibration, individual fairness
  • Mitigation: Pre-processing (reweighting), in-processing (adversarial debiasing), post-processing (threshold adjustment)
  • Tools: AIF360, Fairlearn, Fairness Indicators, Themis-ML
  • Impossibility: Cannot satisfy demographic parity + equalized odds + calibration simultaneously when base rates differ
Technical Guide AIF360

Transparency & Explainability

Making model decisions understandable to stakeholders — from developers to end users to regulators.

  • Post-hoc: SHAP, LIME, Integrated Gradients, Attention visualization, Counterfactuals
  • Intrinsic: Decision trees, rule lists, generalized additive models (GAMs), prototype networks
  • LLM-specific: Chain-of-thought faithfulness, attention probing, mechanistic interpretability (SAEs)
  • Standards: Model Cards, Data Sheets, System Cards, EU AI Act transparency obligations
Technical Guide SHAP

Privacy & Data Protection

Protecting sensitive data throughout the ML lifecycle — training, inference, and deployment.

  • Threats: Membership inference, data extraction, attribute inference, model inversion, reconstruction
  • Defenses: DP-SGD, federated learning, synthetic data, homomorphic encryption, TEEs, PII redaction
  • Regulations: GDPR (Art. 22), EU AI Act, HIPAA, CCPA/CPRA, sector-specific (finance, healthcare)
  • Practical: Zero-retention APIs, local inference, differential privacy budgets, data minimization
Technical Guide Presidio

AI Alignment & Safety

Ensuring AI systems pursue intended goals reliably, even as capabilities scale beyond human supervision.

  • Outer alignment: Reward specification, RLHF/DPO/ORPO, Constitutional AI, RLAIF
  • Inner alignment: Mesa-optimization, deceptive alignment, goal misgeneralization
  • Scalable oversight: Debate, amplification, recursive reward modeling, weak-to-strong generalization
  • Interpretability: SAEs, circuit analysis, automated interpretability, probing
Alignment Overview Interpretability

Robustness & Adversarial Safety

Ensuring models behave correctly under distribution shift, adversarial attacks, and edge cases.

  • Adversarial: PGD, AutoAttack, jailbreaks, prompt injection, backdoors, trojan detection
  • OOD detection: Mahalanobis, energy scores, likelihood ratios, conformal prediction
  • Certified defenses: Randomized smoothing, interval bound propagation, Lipschitz constraints
  • Evaluation: RobustBench, HEXA, AdvGLUE, Red-teaming frameworks (Garak, PromptInject)
Technical Guide RobustBench

Governance, Regulation & Standards

Policy frameworks, compliance, risk management, and organizational processes for responsible AI.

  • Regulations: EU AI Act (risk tiers), US Executive Order, NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, sector rules
  • Risk tiers: Unacceptable (banned), High-risk (conformity assessment), Limited, Minimal
  • Processes: Model risk management, incident reporting, audit trails, human oversight
  • Standards: IEEE 7000, ISO/IEC 23894, NIST AI RMF 1.0, OECD AI Principles
Governance Guide NIST AI RMF

Societal Impact & Ethics

Broader consequences — labor displacement, inequality, environmental cost, misuse, dual-use.

  • Labor: Automation risk, augmentation vs replacement, reskilling, universal basic compute
  • Environment: Training/inference carbon footprint, water usage, hardware supply chain
  • Misuse: Deepfakes, disinformation, cyberattacks, surveillance, autonomous weapons
  • Equity: Digital divide, Global South access, language bias, cultural representation
