History

The History of AI

From Turing's dream to GPT-7 — seven decades of breakthroughs, winters, and revolutions that shaped artificial intelligence.

1950–1970

The Origins & First Hopes

1950

Turing Test Proposed

Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing the Imitation Game as a measure of machine intelligence.

1956

Dartmouth Conference

The term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined at a summer workshop at Dartmouth College. Attendees include John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, and Claude Shannon.

1957

Perceptron Invented

Frank Rosenblatt creates the Perceptron — the first neural network capable of learning from data. Demonstrated at Cornell.

1966

ELIZA Chatbot

Joseph Weizenbaum builds ELIZA at MIT — the first natural language processing program, simulating a Rogerian psychotherapist.

1970–1990

Expert Systems & AI Winters

1972

MYCIN Expert System

Stanford develops MYCIN for diagnosing bacterial infections — one of the first successful expert systems, outperforming some doctors.

1974–1980

First AI Winter

Funding cuts after overpromising. Minsky and Papert's "Perceptrons" highlights neural network limitations, shifting focus to symbolic AI.

1986

Backpropagation Popularized

Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams publish on backpropagation, revitalizing neural network research and enabling deep learning.

1988

Reinforcement Learning Revival

Watkins publishes Q-learning. TD-Gammon (1992) shows RL can master backgammon at near-human level.

1990–2012

Statistical Learning & the Deep Learning Revolution

1997

Deep Blue Defeats Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue becomes the first computer to beat a reigning world chess champion, demonstrating brute-force search + evaluation.

1998

MNIST & LeNet

LeCun releases the MNIST dataset and LeNet — convolutional neural networks achieve practical handwritten digit recognition.

2006

Deep Belief Networks

Hinton shows deep networks can be pre-trained layer-by-layer, sparking the modern deep learning renaissance.

2009–2012

ImageNet & AlexNet

ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC) launches in 2009. AlexNet wins in 2012, cutting error rates by 10% — the moment deep learning goes mainstream.

2013–2022

Transformers, LLMs & Generative AI

2014

GANs & Attention Mechanism

Goodfellow introduces GANs. Bahdanau proposes attention for machine translation — laying the groundwork for transformers.

2017

"Attention Is All You Need"

Vaswani et al. at Google introduce the Transformer architecture — the most consequential ML paper of the decade. Self-attention replaces recurrence.

2018–2020

GPT & BERT Era

GPT-1 (2018), BERT (2018), GPT-2 (2019), GPT-3 (2020). Pre-training + fine-tuning paradigm dominates NLP. Few-shot learning emerges.

2021–2022

DALL-E, ChatGPT & Diffusion

OpenAI's DALL-E (2021), Stable Diffusion (2022), and ChatGPT (Nov 2022) bring generative AI to the mainstream. AI goes viral.

2023–2025

The Agent Era & Multimodal Models

2023

GPT-4, Claude, Gemini & Open Source

GPT-4 (Mar 2023), Claude 2, Gemini (Dec 2023), Llama 2 open-source. Context windows grow to 100K+ tokens. Multimodal capabilities expand rapidly.

2024

Agents, Reasoning & Efficiency

Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4o, Llama 3, Gemini 1.5 (1M context). AI agents gain traction. Reasoning models (o1, DeepSeek-R1). Efficient inference via quantization, speculation.

2025

Multimodal Agents & Research Automation

Claude 4, GPT-4.5, Gemini 2.0. Native multimodal models. Code agents solve real engineering tasks. AI-for-science breakthroughs accelerate. Global AI regulation frameworks emerge.

2026+
