Role Deep Dives

AI Job Roles Breakdown

What each role does, required skills, typical background, and compensation ranges.

ML Research Scientist

Novel algorithms, architectures, theory. Publishes at NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR. PhD typically required.

  • Skills: Deep math (optimization, probability, linear algebra), PyTorch/JAX, paper implementation, research taste
  • Background: PhD (CS, Math, Physics), strong publication record
  • Comp (US): $250k–$600k+ (base + equity + bonus)
  • Top employers: DeepMind, OpenAI, Anthropic, FAIR, Google Brain, MSR, NVIDIA Research
Full Guide Resources

ML Engineer (Applied)

Productionize models: pipelines, serving, monitoring, optimization, MLOps. Strong SWE + ML.

  • Skills: Python, Docker/K8s, TFX/Kubeflow, MLflow, feature stores, distributed training, ONNX/TensorRT
  • Background: CS/Eng degree, SWE experience, ML coursework or self-taught
  • Comp (US): $200k–$450k
  • Top employers: Uber, Airbnb, Netflix, Meta, Google, Amazon, fintech, autonomous vehicles
Full Guide MLOps Resources

AI Engineer / LLM Engineer

Build applications on foundation models: prompting, RAG, agents, fine-tuning, eval, deployment.

  • Skills: LangGraph/LangChain, vector DBs, OpenAI/Anthropic APIs, eval frameworks, prompt engineering, fine-tuning (LoRA)
  • Background: Full-stack SWE, ML literacy, product mindset, fast learner
  • Comp (US): $180k–$350k
  • Demand: Exploding — every company needs LLM integration
Full Guide LLM Eng Handbook

AI Product Manager

Define AI product strategy, work with research/eng, manage non-deterministic systems, responsible AI.

  • Skills: ML literacy (not coding), experiment design, eval metrics, UX for AI, regulation awareness, stakeholder management
  • Background: PM experience + ML coursework/cert, or former ML eng transitioning
  • Comp (US): $200k–$400k
  • Key diff from traditional PM: Probabilistic outputs, data flywheels, eval-driven dev, safety/ethics
Full Guide a16z Guide

MLOps / Platform Engineer

Build the infrastructure: training clusters, serving platforms, feature stores, CI/CD for ML.

  • Skills: Kubernetes, Ray, Kubeflow, MLflow, Feast, Terraform, GPU scheduling, distributed systems
  • Background: DevOps/SRE + ML interest, or ML eng moving to infrastructure
  • Comp (US): $180k–$350k
  • Trend: Platform teams enabling 100+ ML engineers
Full Guide Awesome MLOps

Data Scientist (ML-heavy)

Experimentation, causal inference, modeling tabular data, business impact, stakeholder communication.

  • Skills: XGBoost/LightGBM, SQL, A/B testing, causal inference, stats, storytelling, Tableau/Looker
  • Background: Quantitative (Stats, Econ, Physics, CS), strong business intuition
  • Comp (US): $150k–$280k
  • Evolution: Moving toward ML engineering for production work
Full Guide Applied ML

AI Safety / Alignment Researcher

Interpretability, scalable oversight, robustness, governance. High impact, competitive entry.

  • Skills: Mechanistic interpretability (SAEs), RLHF/RLAIF, debate, weak-to-strong, eval design, red-teaming
  • Background: PhD or exceptional portfolio (MATS, ARENA, independent research)
  • Comp (US): $200k–$500k (varies widely by org)
  • Orgs: Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind, Apollo, METR, FAR AI, CAIS, CHAI, GovAI
Full Guide Alignment Forum

Learning Curricula

Structured paths from zero to job-ready — pick your starting point.

🎓 Complete Beginner (0–6 months)

  1. Python + NumPy/Pandas (freeCodeCamp, 20h)
  2. ML Foundations — Andrew Ng Coursera (3 courses, ~3 months)
  3. Hands-On ML with Scikit-Learn/Keras (Géron, Ch 1–8)
  4. Kaggle: Titanic → House Prices → Spaceship Titanic
  5. Fast.ai Practical Deep Learning (free, top-down)
  6. Build 3 portfolio projects end-to-end
Detailed Curriculum

🚀 SWE → AI Engineer (2–4 months)

  1. LLM Fundamentals: tokenization, transformers, APIs (1 week)
  2. Prompt Engineering: CoT, few-shot, structured output, DSPy (1 week)
  3. RAG: LangGraph/LlamaIndex, vector DBs, eval (RAGAS) (2 weeks)
  4. Agents: ReAct, tools, memory, multi-agent (2 weeks)
  5. Fine-tuning: LoRA/QLoRA with Unsloth, eval (2 weeks)
  6. Deploy: vLLM/Ollama, observability, guardrails (1 week)
  7. Capstone: Ship a real AI product
GenAI Deep Dive

🔬 Research Track (6–12+ months)

  1. Math: Linear Algebra (3B1B), Calculus, Probability (StatQuest)
  2. Deep Learning: fast.ai + Dive into Deep Learning (D2L) book
  3. Transformers: Annotated Transformer, nanoGPT, BERT/GPT from scratch
  4. Reproduce 3–5 papers (ICLR/NeurIPS/ICML recent)
  5. Original project → ArXiv/Workshop/Conference submission
  6. MATS/ARENA/SERI MATS programs, research internships
